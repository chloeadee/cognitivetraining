Frequently Asked Questions

We understand that this type of cognitive training may be new to you! Here are some frequently asked questions and their answers.


Am I doing this right?

There is no right or wrong way to participate in these techniques. Everyone will have a different experience. For some, participating in these exercises may be tough and might require lots of mental effort. Other people may find it easy to follow along! Each session can be enjoyable for some people, but may be boring.

As long as you are following instructions to the best of your ability, it is likely that you are doing it right!


I’m always distracted! Am I really bad at this?

Many people will begin these cognitive training sessions and assume they aren’t doing well because they are always distracted. It’s important to note that noticing that you are distracted is an excellent starting point!

Your attention is like a muscle. Every time you notice a distraction and redirect your attention back to the task at hand, its like repeating an exercise in the gym. The more you can notice your distraction, the more opportunities you have to strengthen your attention muscles. Practice makes perfect!

After a while, you might find that you are able to maintain focus for longer, and can more effectively refocus your attention when you notice distractions.


How I am supposed to feel?

How you feel during the training sessions may be different depending on your mood, time of the day, fatigue or the type of session you complete. There is no one ‘correct’ way you should feel.

It is normal to feel calm, tired or relaxed after practicing these techniques. Paying attention to the breath, increases activity in the parasympathetic nervous system (known as the rest and digest system) which leaves the body feeling relaxed.

These exercises also require you to engage your attention and as a result, some people can feel energised, sharp and focused afterwards. All experiences are valid.


Why do I have to do this three times a day?

Research shows that brief, regular cognitive training sessions like these can help to improve cognitive performance and well-being and are more effective than a single longer session.

This is why we encourage you to partake in the exercises three times per day. As they are short, we hope that it is easy for you to engage on a regular basis throughout the week.


When should I do the training?

There is not a single ‘correct’ time to practice. Depending how you feel during the exercises, you may feel like they are best completed just before you go to bed at night (as they make you feel relaxed) or perhaps when you wake up in the morning (as you feel energised). It might be a great tool to break up your day at work or university.

People get the best results when they incorporate the practice into their regular, daily routines. For example, you might decide to do your training sessions just after you brush your teeth in the morning, just before lunch, and then just before bed. Pairing this new task with others that are your daily habits will ensure that you are not forgetting to complete them!
